# Slide gate

## Hard gates

- If the Point contains at least three structural actors/stages or two relationships, a meaning-bearing diagram is mandatory.
- When mandatory, the diagram is the dominant visual and occupies at least half of the core content region.
- Every required node, edge, boundary, and direction is represented and checked.
- A context-free target reader can paraphrase the headline and identify input, decision maker, action, and outcome in a ten-second cold read.
- The technical executive can state the so-what, decision, principal risk or constraint, and next action.
- Point coverage is complete; factual caveats do not displace the core explanation.
- Rendering and traceability hard checks are zero.
- When a baseline exists, mental-model reconstruction, scanability, and information coverage do not regress.

## Scored gate

Score every criterion from 1 to 5. A score below 4 fails. Weighted total must be at least 90.

| Review | Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|---:|
| Diagram | point_coverage | 20 |
| Diagram | structural_visibility | 20 |
| Diagram | diagram_accuracy | 15 |
| Executive | first_glance_takeaway | 15 |
| Reader | reader_comprehension | 10 |
| Visual QA | hierarchy | 10 |
| Visual QA | traceability_qa | 10 |

Visual QA hard checks are semantic_drift, overflow, unintended_overlap, clipping, broken_or_unreadable_text, diagram_direction_errors, and missing_material_sources. Each must equal zero.

No diagram cannot receive a diagram score when the hard gate requires one. Clean rendering cannot compensate for weak meaning, missing structure, or failed teach-back.
